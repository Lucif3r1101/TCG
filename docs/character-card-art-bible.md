# Chronicles of the RIFT: Character Lore Bible

## World Premise

The Rift is a living fracture in reality. It leaks memory, time, heat, beasts, and dead echoes into the world. Six legendary champions became the final line between civilization and collapse. Each champion forged a faction, and each faction carries a 52-card doctrine of war.

## The Six Champions

### 1. Riftforged Sentinel

Role: Control / Fortress

The Sentinel was once chief architect of the Bastion Gates, the shield network protecting the last stable city. He discovered the shield matrix was built on falsified readings. The city leadership hid that the Rift had already breached the lower districts.

He chose rebellion over obedience. He tore down the false wall and rebuilt a true defensive lattice from salvaged core-metal and memory-reactive light.

Core themes:
- Duty versus truth
- Protection through sacrifice
- Tactical patience and denial

Visual symbols:
- Hard-light hex barriers
- Citadel armor and shield drones
- Cyan-white defensive energy

### 2. Void Ranger

Role: Tempo / Precision

The Ranger hunted Rift anomalies before they manifested. Repeated phase-jumps split her identity into alternate timeline fragments. In some timelines she became a pure hunter; in others, she vanished while tracking her own echo.

Her path is reintegration. She no longer chases speed for its own sake. She now strikes with intent, using every timeline shard as discipline.

Core themes:
- Identity under fragmentation
- Speed with control
- Precision pressure and tactical burst

Visual symbols:
- Vector trails and phase distortions
- Indigo-cyan contrails
- Long-range precision gear

### 3. Ember Arcanist

Role: Combo / Chain Magic

The Arcanist was the brightest scholar of ember-core spellcraft. During a forbidden rite, her uncontrolled chain casting burned an entire district. She survived by binding ember residue directly into her nervous system.

Now she treats magic as architecture. Every cast is part of a sequence. Every sequence has a cost.

Core themes:
- Genius and consequence
- Risk versus mastery
- Escalation and combo discipline

Visual symbols:
- Runic circles and molten glyphs
- Ember-orange and magenta surges
- Arcane lattice formations

### 4. Ironbound Beastmaster

Role: Swarm / Board Pressure

The Beastmaster unified engineered war-beasts and frontier clans through a neural bond network. Sabotage shattered the network mid-campaign, turning allied packs feral and forcing brutal retreats.

He rebuilt leadership around trust, ritual, and earned bonds instead of forced control implants.

Core themes:
- Command through loyalty
- Instinct and structure
- Overwhelm via coordinated packs

Visual symbols:
- Iron harnesses and primal musculature
- Rust-brass war gear
- Claw impact motifs

### 5. Chronomancer

Role: Utility / Turn Control

The Chronomancer could read branching futures, but each rewrite erased personal memory. He saved cities and lost names, faces, and years of his own life.

After becoming trapped in a recursive failure loop, he abandoned perfect outcomes. He now bends time to create opportunity, not certainty.

Core themes:
- Foresight and burden
- Control versus acceptance
- Delay, replay, and initiative theft

Visual symbols:
- Clock halos and stasis shards
- Gold-azure temporal arcs
- Echoed silhouettes

### 6. Abyss Revenant

Role: Drain / Attrition

The Revenant was a fallen champion revived by soul-binding in the abyssal trenches. Every battle sustains him and corrupts him. He fears the day survival fully replaces humanity.

His purpose is now clear: guard the boundary where death, memory, and return converge.

Core themes:
- Humanity versus hunger
- Vengeance transformed into guardianship
- Endurance through siphon and recursion

Visual symbols:
- Soul wisps and chain sigils
- Violet-crimson necro energy
- Fragmented black armor

## Faction Relationships

- Sentinel and Chronomancer are strategic allies but clash on ethics.
- Ranger and Arcanist cooperate in strike operations but disagree on risk tolerance.
- Beastmaster distrusts institutional command; respects Sentinel’s frontline honor.
- Revenant is feared by all factions yet repeatedly saves collapse zones none can hold.

## Card Identity Doctrine

Each faction’s 52 cards must feel like one military-religious language:
- Units (`c01-c32`) show the faction’s living force structure.
- Spells (`c33-c52`) show its doctrine, rituals, and battlefield philosophy.
- `c52` is always the faction’s mythic climax image.

## Tone Rules

- No comedy tone in card lore.
- No modern slang in official flavor text.
- Every card should imply battlefield purpose and narrative stakes.

## Canon Timeline (High-Level)

1. Rift anomalies become permanent.
2. Bastion governance covers up true breach depth.
3. Champions diverge into separate doctrines.
4. First convergence war creates the six faction identities.
5. Present day: tabletop conflicts represent decisive engagements around Rift nodes.

## Authoring Rule

When expanding lore, every new card must answer:
1. What does this card protect, hunt, reshape, command, delay, or consume?
2. Which champion philosophy does it reinforce?
3. Why is this image unmistakably from this faction?
